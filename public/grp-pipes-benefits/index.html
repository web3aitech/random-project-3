GRP Pipes Benefits

Why GRP

The advantages of GRP pipes

Light weight

GRP is only ¼ the weight of ductile iron pipe and around 1/10 the weight of concrete pipe.

Corrosion resistance

Long, effective service life with no cathodic protection needed; hydraulic characteristics essentially unchanged over time.

Extremely smooth bore

Low friction loss means less pumping energy, and less slime build-up lowers cleaning cost.

Low maintenance

Easy to maintain, does not rust, easily cleaned, and requires minimal or no protection from the environment.

Electric properties

Non-conductive; conductive pipes can be produced to dissipate static-electricity buildup when transporting certain fluids.

Long pipe sections

Standard length up to 12 m means fewer joints, reducing installation time and cost.

Dimensional stability

Maintains the critical tolerances required for the most demanding structural and piping applications.

Material properties

Meets the most stringent requirements for chemical resistance and mechanical strength.

Double-bell coupling joints

Manufactured using the same process and material; easy to joint, tight, eliminates infiltration/exfiltration, and allows flexible alignment.

Easy alteration

Pipes can be cut and jointed to form any fitting - elbows at any angle, tees with different branches, reducers - and edges modified to suit other materials.

GRP pipe applications

GRP pipes can be used for:

  • Water transmission and distribution (potable and raw water)
  • Sanitary sewerage collection system (gravity flow and force main)
  • Storm water
  • Effluent water
  • Seawater intake and outfalls
  • Circulating cooling water, make-up and blowdown lines for power plants
  • Industrial application
  • Irrigation
  • Odor-control piping
  • Oil-field injection piping
  • Chemical process lines
  • Fire-fighting lines
  • Ducting
  • Slip lining for pipe rehabilitation
  • Insulated pipes for district-cooling lines (core pipe and jacket)

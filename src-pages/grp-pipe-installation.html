GRP Pipe Installation

Installation methods

Above ground or underground

Pipes can be installed above ground using a self-restrained joint such as hand lay-up lamination, or underground using the regular double-bell coupling as the standard joint. Underground installation can be by the traditional open-trench method, or by micro-tunneling or jacking.

Pipe joint

Standard GRP pipe jointing systems include: Double Bell Reka Coupling, Hand Lay Up, Flange, Flexible Steel Coupling, and Mechanical Steel Coupling. The double-bell Reka coupling is the ideal joint normally offered for non-restrained systems, mainly used for underground applications. Lamination or flange is the typical joint for restrained systems, used for above-ground applications and pumping-station areas.

The underground installation sequence

From trench preparation to deflection verification - the standard buried-pipe method using double-bell Reka couplings.

  1. 01

    Excavate & bed

    Open trench to grade; place and compact the bedding layer to support the pipe uniformly.

  2. 02

    Lay & joint

    Lower pipe into the trench; assemble the double-bell Reka coupling for a self-restrained, infiltration-free joint.

  3. 03

    Backfill & compact

    Place and compact initial backfill around the pipe haunches to develop the required soil stiffness.

  4. 04

    Verify deflection

    Measure vertical deflection against the design limit before final backfill and acceptance.

Production & supply of GRP pipe systems

Dubai Pipes plant is for the production and supply of Glass Reinforced Plastic (GRP) Pipes, Joints, Fittings, Manhole Liners, Cover Slabs, and Tanks. GRP pipes are produced by the Continuous winding process - using know-how and machinery supplied by Flowtite Technology AS - or by the Dual Helical winding process.

Pipes are produced with a diameter range up to 2400 mm, pressure rating up to 32 bar, Specific Tangential Initial Stiffness of 2500, 5000 and 10000 N/m2, and a manufactured standard length of up to 12 m. Our GRP pipes are in accordance with international standards including AWWA, BS EN and ASTM.

Anatomy of a GRP pipe

Every pipe is built from three engineered layers, each with a job. The resin-rich liners protect; the structural wall carries the load.

  • 01 - Inner liner - resin-rich; chemical resistance and a smooth bore, up to 2.5 mm.
  • 02 - Structural layer - the majority of wall thickness; mechanical strength.
  • 03 - Outer liner - resin-rich; environmental protection.

Why conventional pipe materials fall short

Steel, ductile iron and concrete each carry a hidden cost over a pipeline's life - a cost GRP removes at the source.

They corrode. Steel and ductile iron rust; concrete leaches and needs linings or cathodic protection that eventually fail. GRP is inherently corrosion-resistant - no lining, no protection current.

They're heavy. Concrete and steel pipe demand heavy-lift equipment, long trench-open time and large crews. GRP is roughly a quarter the weight of steel - faster to handle, joint and lay.

They lose flow. As conventional pipe ages it tuberculates and scales, dropping capacity and raising pumping cost. GRP's smooth bore keeps its hydraulic efficiency for the life of the line.

No lining. No corrosion current. No flow loss. GRP removes the hidden cost at the source.

How a Dubai Pipes GRP pipe is made

From incoming raw material to a hydrostatically-tested finished pipe - a controlled, repeatable process under our documented quality system.

  1. 01

    Raw material

    Glass-fibre rovings, thermoset resin and silica sand - inspected on receipt against specification.

  2. 02

    Winding

    Continuous-filament or reciprocal helical winding on a mandrel builds the liner, structural wall and outer liner in one pass.

  3. 03

    Cure & calibrate

    The resin cures and the pipe is calibrated to its outside diameter, then cut to its 12 m standard length.

  4. 04

    Hydrostatic test

    Every pipe is pressurised to its rated pressure before release - the first line of conformance verification.

  5. 05

    Joints & finish

    Couplings, fittings and manhole liners are fitted; the pipe is prepared for above-ground or buried installation.
